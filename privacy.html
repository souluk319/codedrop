CodeDrop Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-06-11

CodeDrop is a web-based coding terminology typing game operated by Kugnus. This policy also covers the private KUGNUS-BRAIN review and policy pages hosted by the same developer for Meta Threads API review.

Data Collected By CodeDrop

CodeDrop may collect a nickname, hashed password, score, WPM, accuracy, selected difficulty, selected word pack, and leaderboard timestamp. Passwords are hashed and are not stored in plain text.

Data Collected By KUGNUS-BRAIN Threads Review Flow

KUGNUS-BRAIN is a private single-user knowledge assistant. If Meta grants Threads profile discovery access, it may retrieve public Threads username data, public post text, public post timestamps, public permalinks, public media metadata, and public engagement counts for owner-selected public Threads profiles.

How Data Is Used

CodeDrop data is used for account access, gameplay, and leaderboards. KUGNUS-BRAIN Threads data is used only for private summarization, source-linked research notes, and owner-only notifications.

Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data. CodeDrop leaderboard entries are visible in the game. KUGNUS-BRAIN research notes are private to the owner and are not publicly redistributed.

Retention And Deletion

CodeDrop users may delete their account through the in-app withdrawal flow or request deletion by email. KUGNUS-BRAIN local data can be deleted by the owner at any time. See Data Deletion Instructions.
